Poster 1

This was my first design and also first introduction into InDesign. I decided to go for a fairly simple yet still appealing design. The content is split up by an cartoon image of bristol. I have used a contrast in text size to ensure the reader see's the appropriate information first.

The title is in a large and bold font to make it clear what the poster is about. The date of the event is located at the top of the page so it is easy to find. The repetition of balloons up the right hand side give depth affect to the poster and makes it stand out more.
For my second poster i have used a bilateral design to align the text. The text is aligned centrally with the most important information decending from the top of the page. I have broken up my backround image to give a bit of contrast and proximity to my content.

Information about the festival is vertically centered in a black box seperated from the logos at the top and bottom of the page. Arranging the poster like this makes it easier for the viewer to group together content.

There is an orange colour scheme throughout my design. I have repeated this by highlighting key words and using lines to further break up the center content.

Poster 3

For my third design i have used a background image of various hot air balloons on a sky blue gradient. I have placed my text around the ballons to make sure its still easily readable.

Most text follows the same typeface and is aligned to the left, apart from 'free and accessable for everyone'. For this i have repeated and aligned the text centrally to ensure it stands out.

I split up the main title and used a different background image of Bristol to add a bit of character. I also scaled down te opacity of te image to create a bit of contrast to the main poster.
